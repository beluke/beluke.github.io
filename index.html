About Me

I am a GIS Analyst for the Geospatial Analysis and Services Program (GRASP) at the Centers for Disease Control (CDC)/Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry (ATSDR), where I develop and manage GIS solutions to support public health initiatives.

With experience supporting regional and federal government, I specialize in helping organizations streamline operations for maintaining and using spatial data effectively. I hold a Master's in Spatial Data Science from Pennsylvania State University and am both a GISP and Part 107 sUAS certified.

As a former teacher and passionate advocate for education and advancement of the geospatial field, I serve as a mentor and volunteer for the Women in GIS mentorship committee.
